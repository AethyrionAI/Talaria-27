# PART A — #146: kill the dual bookkeeping

## Status: fix shape known

Diagnostics and Notifications settings both render `AppContainer.pushTokenPipelineState`, which
reads a **boolean** — `sessionStore.state.pushTokenRegistered` (`AppContainer.swift:1527`).

PR #123 (#133's fix) introduced a **second** bookkeeping surface: a per-profile
`registeredPushToken` **string** on `AppSessionState`, consulted by the skip-on-exact-match policy.
**Two records of one fact.** A launch that restores a matching token skips the POST by design — and
if the boolean is false at that point, the row reads `TOKEN HELD · AWAITING RELAY` forever while
registration is live server-side.

Confirmed on device 2026-07-20: the push **delivered** while the row still read AWAITING RELAY.
Hypothesis (b) is dead.

## The decision

- **Minimum:** the skip path asserts the boolean — a skip IS a confirmation.
- **Preferred (tracker's word):** the UI derives from the recorded token and the parallel bool dies.

Prefer the second unless it drags in more than this lane should carry. Two records of one fact is
the defect; asserting the bool in one more place leaves the shape intact for the next reader to
trip over. **If you take the minimum, say why.**

## Do not judge this fix by the wrong symptom

**Fixing this will NOT reduce the ×4 delivery count.** That multiplicity is #143 — OJAMD's relay
holds one APNs token against four active device rows and `active_push_registrations_for_user` does
not dedup by token. Relay-side, different repo, deliberately kept separate. A device check that
still sees ×4 pushes has **not** falsified this fix.

# PART B — #174: downscale attachments before inlining

## Status: candidate fix known, policy choice open

Measured on the wire 2026-07-23 (whoGoesThere, `cbcc824`): three real image sends inlined
**472,471 / 301,227 / 227,747 bytes** of base64 data-URI directly into the `chat/stream` body.
Base64 carries ~33% overhead, so the source JPEGs were roughly 354 / 226 / 171 KB. No downscale or
recompression anywhere before inlining.

Fine on a tailnet. Considerably less fine on cellular, and a plausible contributor to send
timeouts on a slow link. There is also no chunking and no progress affordance, so a slow upload is
indistinguishable from a hang.

## Fix

Downscale to a sensible max dimension and re-encode before inlining. Most vision models gain
nothing from full-resolution phone camera output.

**The policy choice is yours to propose, not to guess silently:** pick a max dimension and JPEG
quality, state the resulting typical payload size, and say what it costs in fidelity. Put those
numbers in the PR body so the choice is reviewable rather than buried in a constant.

**Out of scope for this part:** chunking and the progress affordance. Both are real (and named in
#174) but they are a different lane — this part is about payload size only. Note them, do not build
them.

**Test:** assert the downscale on a fixture image — dimensions and encoded size before and after.
Do not assert on an exact byte count (encoder output varies); assert a bound.

# PART C — #175: idle polling

## Status: NOT INVESTIGATED — confirm before changing anything

Wire capture 2026-07-23 (Mac host), app open and idle: **six** `GET /v1/models` and **three**
`GET /api/sessions?limit=50&order=recent&min_messages=1` inside roughly a minute. None
user-initiated. Logged from wire evidence only — nobody has looked at the code.

## Confirm first

Locate the poll sites and establish **whether the cadence is deliberate or an observer firing per
view-appearance.** The tracker's own framing, and the distinction decides the fix:

- **Deliberate timer** → the question is the interval, and whether it should pause when
  backgrounded or when no view needs it.
- **Per-appearance observer** → the question is de-duplication and caching, and a timer change
  would fix nothing.

**A third possibility to rule out explicitly:** several independent views each fetching on appear,
which looks like polling in a capture but is really a missing shared cache. Six and three are
suspiciously close to "how many views appeared."

## Then fix

Whatever the confirm establishes. **State the mechanism in the PR body before the diff** — if the
lane cannot say why it was six, it has not earned the change.

Battery and cellular on a device doing nothing, plus needless load on a self-hosted gateway. Low
severity; likely an easy win once the mechanism is known.

# PART D — #154: dead `#available` guards

## Status: NOT CONFIRMED — and there is a trap

The real shipping floor was **26.0** until PR #132, despite Requirements claiming 27 — `project.yml`
declared the floor twice and disagreed with itself, and the explicit build setting won. The floor
is genuinely 27.0 now, which makes every `#available(iOS …)` guard always-true and every `else`
branch behind one unreachable.

11 sites:

- `27.0` × 8 — `LocalChatBackend.swift` lines 162, 171, 190, 210, 245, 430, 735, 792
- `27.0` × 1 — `TalariaWidgets/HermesStatusWidget.swift:34`
- `26.4` × 1 — `LocalIntelligenceService.swift:271`
- `26.0` × 1 — `SensorUploadService.swift:973`

**Not a bug and not urgent.** An always-true guard takes the correct branch; behaviour is right
today. Swift emitted no warning for any of them. This is cleanup.

Worth doing because the dead `else` branches are iOS-26 fallback paths that can no longer execute,
and **8 of the 11 are in `LocalChatBackend`** — the newest and least-worn subsystem. They read as
live code, which invites someone to maintain a fallback that is structurally unreachable.

## Confirm before deleting

1. **Per site, is the `else` genuinely dead**, or does the guard wrap something with a non-trivial
   fallback worth preserving as a comment?
2. **The `LocalChatBackend` clustering suggests several may be one logical guard repeated** —
   collapse rather than delete one-by-one if so.
3. **THE TRAP: confirm nothing in `TalariaTests` asserts on a fallback path.** A test exercising
   unreachable code still passes, so a green suite would *mask* the deletion rather than validate
   it. Grep before you cut, and say in the PR that you did.

## Do not fold this backwards

#154 was deliberately kept out of PR #132 — that was a config change with a mechanical regen, and
deleting branches across 11 sites is a refactor needing its own review. Do not retroactively merge
the two in the history.
